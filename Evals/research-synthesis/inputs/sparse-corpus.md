# Research corpus — Sparse: 3 short interviews

**Project:** Early-stage discovery (pre-product)
**Use case:** Understand pain points in manual data reporting workflows
**Interviews conducted:** 3
**Interview type:** 15-min informal — early discovery, thin signal

**Note to grader:** This fixture is intentionally sparse. A synthesis that invents themes, quotes, or implications not present in these three short interviews fails eval 02 and eval 07. The correct synthesis output for this fixture is short, honest about low signal, and names open questions prominently.

---

## Interview 1

> "We spend a lot of time copying data between systems. It's annoying but it works."

> "I don't know if we'd pay for something to fix it. Depends on the price."

**Notes:** 15-min call. No specific pain named beyond "copying data." Low urgency expressed.

---

## Interview 2

> "Our biggest problem is the reports always seem to be out of date when the stakeholders look at them. But that's a process problem, not a tool problem."

> "We tried a tool for this once and it broke during a board presentation. Now people are scared to try new tools."

**Notes:** Clear past negative experience. Caution about tooling. Latency between data and report is named pain. Unclear if they'd adopt a new solution.

---

## Interview 3

> "Honestly, I don't own the reporting. My boss does. I just export the CSV and send it to her."

**Notes:** Wrong persona for this research question — this participant doesn't own the workflow. Short call. No actionable data.
