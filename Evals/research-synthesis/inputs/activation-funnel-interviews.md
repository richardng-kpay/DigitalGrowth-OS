# Research corpus — Activation funnel discovery interviews

**Project:** [YOUR_ANCHOR_PROJECT]
**Use case:** Understand why new users drop off before completing activation (completing their first meaningful action in the product)
**Interviews conducted:** 4
**Interview type:** 30-min qualitative — semi-structured, problem discovery

---

## Interview 1 — New user (week 1, dropped before activation)

**Participant:** New user, joined 12 days ago, has not yet completed the key activation action
**Interviewer question focus:** What did you do in your first session? Where did you get stuck?

> "I signed up because I saw the demo and it looked straightforward. But when I got into the product, I didn't know where to start. The dashboard shows all these metrics but I didn't have any data connected yet, so it just showed zeros everywhere. I poked around for like 10 minutes and then got pulled into a meeting. I haven't been back since."

> "The emails they send are reminders to 'complete your setup' but they don't tell me what setup means for someone in my role. I'm not the one who owns the data pipeline. I needed someone else to do something first, and I didn't know that until I asked our data team."

**Interviewer notes:** User mentioned "zeros everywhere" twice. Clear dependency on data team that onboarding flow doesn't surface. User was motivated at signup — attrition happened at first session friction, not at signup intent.

---

## Interview 2 — Power user (activated within 3 days, heavy daily use)

**Participant:** Power user, activated day 3, logs in 5x/week
**Interviewer question focus:** What made your first week different from other tools you've tried?

> "Honestly the sample data set. I connected the sample data set first and started playing with the features before I had anything real in there. That's how I learned what it was even for. Most tools make you connect your real data first and then you're staring at a blank screen."

> "I almost didn't connect real data either. The permissions flow was confusing. But by then I was hooked enough to push through it."

**Interviewer notes:** Sample data was the unlock for this user — they found it on their own, it was not surfaced by onboarding. Power user explicitly compared this product favorably to category peers. Permissions friction acknowledged but power user had enough motivation to push through.

---

## Interview 3 — Operations manager (activated week 3, use is shallow)

**Participant:** Ops manager, activated week 3, logs in 1-2x/week for reports only
**Interviewer question focus:** How do you use the product today? How does it fit into your workflow?

> "My team set it up for me. I just look at the weekly report they send. I've never actually logged in and done anything myself — I just read the export."

> "I guess I'm not really your target user. I don't have time to learn a new tool. If it fits into my existing workflow it works, if it doesn't, I ignore it."

**Interviewer notes:** This user did not self-activate — they were activated by a team member. "Activation" for this persona is different: it means the product fits into existing reporting workflow, not that they complete a first action in the UI. Ops manager is influencer/approver, not daily user.

---

## Interview 4 — Customer Success Manager (incomplete activation, strong intent)

**Participant:** CSM, has connected data but has not used the core workflow feature
**Interviewer question focus:** You connected data but haven't used [core feature] — what got in the way?

> "I connected the data and I was excited. But then [core feature] has all these configuration options and I don't know which ones are relevant to me. There's no 'start here for a CSM' path. I've been meaning to find time to figure it out."

> "I watched the help video but it was 8 minutes and showed a use case that wasn't mine. I need something that just says: here's what you do first if you're a CSM who cares about renewal risk."

**Interviewer notes:** Data connected but core workflow unused. High intent, blocked by configuration complexity and generic help content. Role-specific guidance is the blocker. This user would be a strong candidate for a role-specific activation path.
