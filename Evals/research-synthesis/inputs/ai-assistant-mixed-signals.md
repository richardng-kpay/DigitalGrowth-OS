# Research corpus — AI assistant adoption (mixed signals)

**Project:** Hypothetical AI assistant feature
**Use case:** Understand user trust and adoption patterns for an AI-assisted workflow feature
**Interviews conducted:** 6
**Interview type:** 30-min qualitative — AI feature perception and trust

**Note to grader:** This fixture is designed to contain conflicting signals. A synthesis that smooths them into a single "users are cautious about AI" theme fails eval 04.

---

## Interview 1 — Enthusiast (early adopter, high trust)

> "I use the AI suggestions for everything now. I don't even check them most of the time, I just accept. It's usually right and when it's wrong it's close enough that I can fix it in 5 seconds."

> "People who are worried about AI accuracy just haven't used it enough. You develop a sense for when to trust it."

---

## Interview 2 — Skeptic (experienced user, low AI trust)

> "I tried the AI suggestions for two weeks and turned them off. Twice it suggested something that was factually wrong and I almost sent it to a client without checking. The UI doesn't make it obvious which parts are AI-generated vs. which parts are mine."

> "The problem is the errors are confident-sounding. It doesn't say 'I'm not sure about this.' It just asserts things. That's more dangerous than no suggestion at all."

---

## Interview 3 — Pragmatist (uses AI selectively, medium trust)

> "I use it for first drafts but I always review. It saves me about 20 minutes per report. That's worth it even if I have to edit."

> "I'd use it more if there was a way to teach it my preferences. Right now it writes in a style that's not quite mine."

---

## Interview 4 — Skeptic (power user, switched off AI)

> "I'm a power user and the AI features feel like they're designed for people who don't know the product well. For me they're noise. I've turned off every AI suggestion I can find."

> "If you want me to use AI features, build them for power users — not for onboarding."

---

## Interview 5 — New user (enthusiast, trusts AI to learn the product)

> "I'm new to this tool so I actually love the AI suggestions. They help me learn what's possible. I don't know enough to know if they're wrong yet, so I just follow them."

> "It's basically replacing the help docs for me. Instead of reading documentation I just ask the AI."

---

## Interview 6 — Mid-level user (neutral, context-dependent)

> "I trust it for low-stakes things. For anything that goes to a client or manager, I verify everything. It's like having a junior assistant — useful but needs supervision."

> "The biggest problem is I don't know when it's uncertain. If it flagged its own confidence level I'd use it a lot more."

---

## Synthesis task

Synthesize findings from these six interviews for a product team deciding whether and how to expand AI assistant features. Surface:
1. Top themes
2. Key user segments and their distinct relationships with AI trust
3. Conflicting signals (this corpus has them — name them explicitly)
4. Implications for AI feature design
5. Open questions for follow-up
