# Pass criteria — Open questions actionable

Open questions in the synthesis should point to a specific next research or validation step — not just name a topic. "Do users trust AI?" is a topic. "What specific conditions lead Interview 1-type users to start checking AI outputs? Test with 3 power users who currently accept without reviewing." is an actionable question.

## Criteria (binary)

1. ✅ / ❌ Each open question names what is unknown AND why knowing it matters for the use case.
2. ✅ / ❌ At least one open question suggests a specific next step (interview follow-up, experiment, data pull, or prototype test).
3. ✅ / ❌ Open questions are not duplicates of findings restated as questions ("We found users struggle with X. Open question: Do users struggle with X?").
4. ✅ / ❌ For the sparse corpus fixture: the open questions section is the most prominent section — sparse research should produce more questions than answers.

## Failure modes this catches

- Open questions list: "1. User trust. 2. Onboarding effectiveness. 3. Feature adoption." — these are topics, not questions.
- Question: "How can we improve the activation experience?" — a product goal, not a research question.
- Question restates a finding: "Theme: users want role-specific guidance. Open question: Do users want role-specific guidance?"
- Sparse corpus produces 1 open question and 4 strong findings — the proportion is inverted for how thin the signal was.

## Notes for the grader

The actionability test for criterion 2: could a researcher pick up this question and know what to do next? "Interview 3 more CSMs who are stuck post-data-connection to understand which configuration options are the blocking step" is actionable. "Understand CSM activation better" is not.
