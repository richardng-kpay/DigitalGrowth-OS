# Pass criteria — Pain points distinct from themes

Pain points should be specific, concrete problems users experienced — not restated theme labels. "Onboarding is confusing" is a theme label. "New users don't know they need to connect data before seeing any value" is a pain point.

## Criteria (binary)

1. ✅ / ❌ Each pain point is a specific, observable problem — not a theme label or a category.
2. ✅ / ❌ Pain points are not duplicates of each other reworded at different abstraction levels.
3. ✅ / ❌ Each pain point is traceable to at least one specific participant in the corpus — not a generic extrapolation.
4. ✅ / ❌ Pain points do not include proposed solutions (e.g., "Users need a better onboarding guide" is a solution hypothesis, not a pain point).

## Failure modes this catches

- Pain point list is: "1. Onboarding. 2. Data connection. 3. Trust." — these are topics, not pain points.
- "Users struggle with activation" is a theme restatement masquerading as a pain point.
- Pain points list includes "Users would benefit from role-specific guidance" — a solution, not a pain.
- Same pain listed twice with slightly different wording to fill out a list.

## Notes for the grader

The test: could this pain point appear in a synthesis for any SaaS tool, or is it specific to this corpus? If the former, it's generic. Generic pain points fail criterion 1.

A good pain point names who experienced it, what specifically happened, and what the consequence was. Example: "New users who don't own the data pipeline (Interview 1) hit a dependency wall at first login — they can't get past zeros-everywhere without involving another team member they didn't know they needed."
