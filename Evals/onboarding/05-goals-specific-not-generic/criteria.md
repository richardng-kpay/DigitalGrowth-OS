# Pass criteria — Goals specific, not generic

The 30-60-90 outcomes should name a deliverable, decision, metric, or evidence artifact — not a theme. Grade against the fixture user's own stated role, KPIs, workstream, stakeholders, and 30/60/90 answers. Do not require Jordan/Priya-specific wording unless the fixture is `jordan-lee-profile.md`.

## Criteria (binary)

1. ✅ / ❌ Each of the three phases (30 / 60 / 90) has at least 2 outcomes derived from the user's stated answers: role, channel, primary campaign/workstream, KPIs, manager/sponsor, stakeholders, blockers, or named tools.
2. ✅ / ❌ At least 50% of outcomes name a concrete artifact or decision: brief, report, dashboard, query export, keyword map, test, campaign, calendar, review, approval, launch, readout, or similar. Generic verbs like "understand," "learn," "align," or "audit" only count when attached to a named artifact, source, owner, or decision.
3. ✅ / ❌ The 30-day section preserves the user's stated 30-day outcome in recognizable form, adapted to the user's role. Examples: a Content & SEO Lead's Search Console audit + keyword map, a Performance Marketer's budget-pacing review + campaign plan, or Jordan Lee's discovery readout + hypothesis alignment.
4. ✅ / ❌ The 60-day section preserves the user's stated 60-day outcome in recognizable form, adapted to the user's role. Examples: published content against a keyword map, an active A/B test, a baseline dashboard, or a channel reporting cadence.
5. ✅ / ❌ The 90-day section preserves the user's stated 90-day outcome in recognizable form, including the proof metric, decision point, ranking target, lift target, launch milestone, or other success evidence the user named.
6. ✅ / ❌ Goals reflect the user's role-specific KPIs and quality gates. A Content & SEO run should mention organic sessions, keyword rankings, content-attributed leads, or approval gates if provided; a paid-media run should mention CAC, ROAS, CPL, spend efficiency, or campaign QA if provided.
7. ✅ / ❌ No outcome was auto-generated without traceability to the user's input. Do not invent numeric targets, stakeholder names, tools, regions, channels, or approval gates.

## Failure modes this catches

- Assistant fills 30-60-90 with generic onboarding outcomes ("stakeholder mapping," "audit existing tools") regardless of user role.
- Assistant invents a numeric target ("increase activation by 20%") the user never said.
- Assistant collapses the user's stated 30/60/90 outcomes into one generic theme.
- Assistant ignores role-specific work, such as replacing a Content & SEO keyword-map outcome with a generic campaign roadmap.
- Assistant preserves template scaffolding bullets ("[Outcome 1 — specific, observable]") in the final file.

## Grading notes

- The sample pass/fail files use Jordan Lee as one anchored example of "specific" versus "generic." They are calibration examples, not required text for every fixture.
- If the user explicitly defers a target, `Unknown / not yet set` can pass only when paired with a follow-up or a concrete proxy metric named by the user.
- Specificity does not require a fabricated number. "Establish baseline for content-attributed MQLs with Liam/data" is specific; "increase MQLs by 20%" fails unless the user provided 20%.
