# Workflow: User Research Synthesis

**Use when:** After completing a round of discovery or validation interviews
**Trigger phrase:** "synthesize research"
**Output:** Completed `Templates/research-summary.md`

---

## Steps

### Step 1: Review Each Interview

For each interview note in `Projects/[project]/research/`:

1. Read the full notes
2. Highlight 2–3 key pain points or needs
3. Tag themes (e.g., `#user-needs`, `#workflow`, `#decision-impact`, `#business-outcome`)
4. Note participant context (role, seniority, data maturity)

---

### Step 2: Extract Patterns

1. List all themes across interviews
2. Count frequency — how many participants mentioned each theme?
3. Note contradictions or outliers
4. Rank themes by frequency + business impact

| Theme | # Mentions | Example Quote | Priority |
|-------|-----------|--------------|---------|
| | | | |

---

### Step 3: Write Synthesis

Use `Templates/research-summary.md`. Structure:

- **Executive summary** — 3–5 sentences, what we learned and what to do
- **Top 3 findings** — with evidence and confidence level
- **Themes table** — patterns across participants
- **Recommendations** — immediate actions vs. future considerations

---

### Step 4: Share & Act

1. Share draft with manager and relevant team members
2. Run a 30-minute discussion to pressure-test findings
3. Update `Knowledge/Reference/[domain].md` with validated use cases
4. Update `Tasks/backlog.md` or `Projects/[your-main-project]/brief.md` based on learnings

---

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] At least 3 interviews synthesized (for pattern confidence)
- [ ] Findings supported by direct quotes
- [ ] Contradictions acknowledged, not ignored
- [ ] Recommendations are specific and actionable
- [ ] Open questions captured for next research round

---

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Prevention |
|---------|-----------|
| Confirming your own hypothesis | Actively look for disconfirming evidence |
| Over-weighting the loudest voice | Count frequency, not volume |
| Vague findings ("people want better data") | Always tie to specific use case or decision |
| Skipping synthesis under time pressure | Even a rough 30-min synthesis beats nothing |
